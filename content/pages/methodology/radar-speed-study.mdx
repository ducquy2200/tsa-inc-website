methodProfile:
  objective: Measure prevailing and high-end vehicle speed behavior to support targeted safety and traffic management decisions.
  tools:
    - Police grade radar speed guns
    - Stationary or mobile radar mounting points
    - Speed distribution analysis templates
    - Compliance assessment reporting format
  processSteps:
    - title: Study objective definition
      description: Set speed analysis goals such as identifying speeding patterns, evaluating interventions, or reviewing compliance.
    - title: Radar deployment
      description: Place radar devices at strategic roadway points to capture representative speed behavior.
    - title: Capture and aggregation
      description: Record speed observations continuously through the approved collection threshold.
    - title: Distribution analysis and reporting
      description: Compile averages, maximums, percentile behavior, and compliance insight into a structured report.
  capturedData:
    - Vehicle speed readings
    - Time-based speed distribution
    - Average and maximum speed indicators
    - Compliance context by location
  analysisOutputs:
    - 85th percentile and distribution analysis
    - Speed hotspot identification
    - Compliance and risk interpretation
    - Strategy-ready report for safety planning
  standards:
    - Radar equipment is configured and checked before data collection.
    - Capture points are selected to represent actual operating behavior.
    - Reporting includes method and location context for interpretation.
  durationRules:
    - Radar speed studies are usually performed for 2 hours or 200 vehicles, whichever comes first.
    - Additional collection windows can be scheduled if the objective requires broader temporal coverage.
